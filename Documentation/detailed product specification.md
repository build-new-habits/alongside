### The Problem We're Solving

**45 million adults in the UK struggle with fitness** - not because they lack motivation, but because traditional fitness apps are built for a mythical "perfect user" who:
- Has unlimited time and energy

- Your energy level today (1-10)

- Your injury/pain levels right now
- Your menstrual cycle phase (if tracking)
- Your life context (stressed, traveling, celebrating)

**The result:** A fitness coach that feels like it truly understands you, not one that makes you feel guilty for being human.

### Why Neurodivergent-First Design Matters

**Neurodivergent users aren't edge cases - they're 15-20% of the population.**

Traditional fitness apps fail neurodivergent users because they:
1. **Assume executive function** - "Just follow the plan" doesn't work when planning is hard
2. **Use shame-based motivation** - Broken streaks trigger anxiety spirals in ADHD users
3. **Lack transparency** - "Why this exercise?" is a genuine need, not a nice-to-have
4. **Exploit dopamine dysregulation** - Variable rewards and FOMO tactics are harmful
5. **Ignore context** - A bad night's sleep or injury makes rigid plans impossible

**Alongside flips this:**
1. **Minimal executive function required** - Daily check-in takes <60 seconds
2. **No shame, ever** - Missing a day loses nothing, fresh start every morning
3. **Transparent rationale** - Every recommendation explains why it was suggested
4. **Ethical rewards** - Credits for real activity, no manipulation
5. **Context-aware** - Adapts to your actual state, not an idealized version of you

### The Science Behind Our Approach

Our design is grounded in evidence-based psychological frameworks:

#### 1. Self-Determination Theory (Deci & Ryan)
**Core Principle:** People are motivated by autonomy, competence, and relatedness - not external pressure.

**How Alongside Applies This:**
- **Autonomy:** You choose which workout to do (3 options daily), or skip entirely
- **Competence:** Exercises matched to your skill level, progressive overload built in
- **Relatedness:** Compassionate coaching language, "we're in this together" tone

#### 2. SOLO Taxonomy (Biggs & Collis)
**Core Principle:** Learning progresses through structured levels from simple to complex understanding.

**How Alongside Applies This:**
- **Unistructural:** Single exercise (e.g., bodyweight squat)
- **Multistructural:** Multiple exercises (e.g., full lower body workout)
- **Relational:** Understanding how exercises connect to goals
- **Extended Abstract:** Creating own workout variations

Exercise recommendations automatically scaffold from simple to complex as you progress.

#### 3. Mood Meter Science (Marc Brackett, Yale RULER)
**Core Principle:** Emotional awareness requires two dimensions - energy and pleasantness.

**How Alongside Applies This:**
- Check-in captures energy (1-10) and mood (1-10) separately
- Exercise recommendations adapt to both dimensions
- Low energy + low mood â†’ Gentle, endorphin-boosting movements
- High energy + low mood â†’ Stress-relief cardio
- Low energy + high mood â†’ Light, enjoyable activities

#### 4. Burnout Research (Dr. Claire Plumbly)
**Core Principle:** Burnout is nervous system dysregulation, detectable through energy patterns.

**How Alongside Applies This:**
- Monitors for 3+ consecutive days with energy â‰¤3
- Activates "Recovery Mode" with gentle-only recommendations
- Message: "You've been running on low energy lately. That's okay - we're focusing on rest."
- No pressure to "push through" - recovery is the priority

**Reference:** *Burnout: How to Manage Your Nervous System Before It Manages You*, Dr. Claire Plumbly, Yellow Kite 2024, ISBN 978-1-399-73342-7

#### 5. Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT) Principles
**Core Principle:** Psychological flexibility and values-based action lead to sustainable behavior change.

**How Alongside Applies This:**
- Exercises aligned with your stated goals and values
- Acceptance of current state ("Low energy is valid, not a failure")
- Commitment to action within current capacity
- No rigid rules, just flexible guidelines

### Women-First Design: Menstrual Cycle Awareness

**51% of the population experiences hormonal fluctuations that traditional fitness apps completely ignore.**

Alongside integrates menstrual cycle science:

**Menstruation Phase (Days 1-5):**
- Energy typically lowest
- Core compression exercises avoided
- Recommendation: Gentle movement, recovery focus

**Follicular Phase (Days 6-14):**
- Energy rising, estrogen increasing
- Optimal for strength building
- Recommendation: Progressive overload, skill development

**Ovulation Phase (Days 15-17):**
- Peak energy and coordination
- Ideal for high-intensity work
- Recommendation: PRs, challenging workouts

**Luteal Phase (Days 18-28):**
- Energy declining, progesterone rising
- Mood may fluctuate
- Recommendation: Steady-state cardio, moderate intensity

**Key Insight:** Women don't need to "push through" hormonal fluctuations. Working WITH your cycle leads to better results and less burnout.

### Men-First Design Too: Hormone Awareness

Alongside doesn't assume all users are the same:
- **Testosterone fluctuations** tracked for male users (morning vs. evening energy)
- **Aging considerations** (40s+ users have different recovery needs)
- **Stress hormones** (cortisol management through recovery recommendations)

### 7. Ethical Reward System (No Manipulation)

**The Problem with Traditional Apps:**
- Streaks create anxiety ("Don't break your streak!")

- FOMO mechanics ("Complete now or lose your bonus!")

**Alongside's Ethical Approach:**

**Credits System:**
- Earn credits for completing exercises (30-120 per exercise)
- Immediate feedback: Confetti animation + credit popup
- NO punishment for missing days (credits never expire or decrease)
- NO duplicate prevention gaming (can't spam same exercise)

**What Credits Are For:**
- Visual progress indicator (gamification without exploitation)
- Future: Unlock bonus content (meal plans, advanced workouts)
- Future: "Sweat Equity" system (earn credits, curb impulse spending)

**No Streaks:**
- NO "X days in a row" pressure
- Instead: "Total active days" (celebrate any movement)
- Missing a day is a fresh start, not a failure

**No Social Comparison:**
- NO leaderboards
- NO "friends are ahead of you" messaging
- NO public workout feeds
- Your journey is private, personal, yours alone

**Why This Matters:**
- **Neurodivergent-friendly:** ADHD users have dopamine dysregulation - variable rewards are harmful
- **Sustainable:** Intrinsic motivation (I feel good) beats external pressure (don't break streak)
- **Compassionate:** Celebrates effort, never punishes humanness

## ğŸ”’ PRIVACY & ETHICS

### Data Privacy Principles

**Phase 1 (Current):**
- **Zero-server architecture** - All data in browser localStorage
- **No accounts** - No registration, email, or identifiers
- **No tracking** - No analytics, no third-party scripts
- **User control** - Export/delete data anytime

**Phase 2 (Commercial):**
- **Opt-in cloud sync** - Users choose to create account
- **End-to-end encryption** - User data encrypted at rest
- **Data portability** - Export to JSON anytime
- **Right to deletion** - One-click permanent deletion
- **GDPR compliant** - Full compliance with data protection

### Ethical Design Commitments

**We Will NEVER:**
1. Use streak pressure to manipulate users
2. Implement social comparison features
3. Use variable ratio reinforcement (slot machine tactics)
4. Send guilt-inducing push notifications
5. Sell user data to third parties
6. Use dark patterns or deceptive design
7. Implement features that exploit dopamine dysregulation
8. Shame users for missing workouts or bad days

**We Will ALWAYS:**
1. Validate low energy/mood as legitimate states
2. Provide transparent rationale for recommendations
3. Respect user autonomy (suggest, never dictate)
4. Protect user privacy (local-first architecture)
5. Design for accessibility (WCAG AAA standards)
6. Center neurodivergent and women's needs
7. Use evidence-based psychological frameworks
8. Prioritize user wellbeing over engagement metrics
